## Best PS4 Control Setup, Camera, and Input Habits

For **NBA The Run ps4** users, control comfort can decide close games. On PS4 hardware, input discipline and camera clarity are more valuable than aggressive sensitivity tweaks. Keep your setup simple, readable, and repeatable.

### Baseline settings to test first

- **Camera:** Medium zoom with wider sideline visibility
- **Defensive assist:** Moderate (not max)
- **Pass target assist:** On, with manual direction practice
- **Vibration:** Optional, but many players prefer off for precision
- **HUD cues:** Keep shot meter and pass indicators visible initially

| Setting | Starter Value | Competitive Adjustment | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Camera Zoom** | **5/10** | 4/10–6/10 | Balance court vision and player detail |
| **Camera Height** | **6/10** | 5/10–7/10 | Improves lane reading |
| **Defensive Assist** | **Medium** | Low-Medium | Encourages manual positioning |
| **Pass Assist** | **On** | On with manual practice | Cleaner fast-break execution |
| **Shot Meter** | **On** | Optional later | Helps early timing consistency |

> **Warning:** If your sticks drift even slightly, avoid high-risk dribble chains until dead-zone settings are stable.

### Input habits that improve quickly

- Use one “safe pass” option every possession.
- Limit cross-court passes unless your feet are set.
- Trigger offense from the wing, then attack weak side.
- On defense, prioritize body positioning before steal attempts.

Most players asking about **NBA The Run ps4** focus on graphics first, but control optimization gives larger performance gains in your first month.
