## In-Match Decision Guide: Offense, Defense, and Momentum

A lot of close games are decided by three things: first pass quality, transition floor balance, and shot selection in the final seconds of the clock.

### Offense rules to apply immediately
- If transition is available, attack early but avoid crowded lanes.
- If the defense sets, shift into spacing and weak-side movement.
- If your star is denied, trigger secondary actions quickly.

### Defense rules that stop scoring runs
- Stop the ball first, then match up.
- Give up fewer corner looks even if that means conceding one extra dribble.
- Rotate from the lowest-value shooter, not from your strongest assignment.

| Game Situation | Bad Habit | Better Choice | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|---|
| **After a made basket** | Sprinting blindly to steal | Set defensive shell first | Prevents easy opponent counters |
| **Late clock offense** | Hero iso from 30 feet | Two-pass action into quality look | Raises shot quality under pressure |
| **Opponent on 6–0 run** | Forcing fast contested 3s | Run one stable set + get a stop | Breaks momentum swing |
| **Your star is trapped** | Holding ball too long | Immediate outlet and cut | Punishes aggressive pressure |

In **NBA The Run ps5**, momentum likely matters as much as ratings. If you control tempo after opponent runs, you’ll steal wins that less organized teams give away.
