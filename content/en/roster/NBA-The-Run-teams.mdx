<Section icon={Swords} title="Knockout Solos vs. Knockout Squads: Team Control Explained">
The primary decision for players entering NBA The Run is how they want to manage their team. **Knockout Solos** and **Knockout Squads** offer fundamentally different control schemes and team dynamics. Understanding these differences is key to forming effective NBA The Run teams.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Knockout Solos">
    - **Control Style**: You build and control your entire team of three players.
    - **Team Setup**: Your pre-selected team of three competes against other player-controlled teams.
    - **Best For**: Players who prefer full strategic control over all team decisions, player switching, offense, and defense.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Knockout Squads">
    - **Control Style**: You control one player on a three-player squad.
    - **Team Setup**: Fill your squad with friends or other online players.
    - **Best For**: Players who enjoy a co-op, role-based experience where each person specializes in one athlete.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Choosing between these modes affects not only your in-game control but also your approach to team synergy and strategy. In Solos, you are responsible for every pass, shot, and defensive rotation for all three players. In Squads, communication and collaboration with your teammates become paramount.

<Callout type="info">
While Knockout Solos offers complete control, Knockout Squads emphasizes teamwork and communication, making it ideal for playing with friends.
</Callout>

</Section>
