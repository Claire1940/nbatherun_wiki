## Advanced Gameplay Notes You Should Practice

The **NBA The Run update** rewards cleaner fundamentals more than flashy inputs. Here are habits that consistently transfer to wins:

- **Pre-pass positioning:** Move before receiving so you catch with momentum.
- **Shot selection discipline:** Don’t force guard-penalty jumpers when a reset is available.
- **Stamina awareness:** Sprinting without purpose hurts late-possession quality.
- **Defensive angle control:** Shade ball handlers toward your help, not open space.
- **Takeover timing:** Trigger momentum boosts in high-leverage possessions, not random ones.

### Mistakes to remove immediately

| Common Mistake | Why It Hurts | Better Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| Spamming contested pull-ups | Contest scaling lowers consistency | Use drive-kick or relocate into cleaner windows |
| Running 3 pure scorers | No defensive identity | Add at least one lockdown or rim protector |
| Ignoring aura/takeover pacing | You lose momentum races | Track who charges takeover fastest on your roster |
| Blindly chasing steals | Missed steal attempts open lanes | Time steals with body position and help nearby |

If you only adopt one adjustment from this **NBA The Run update** guide, make it this: build lineups that can both score and survive defensive possessions. That balance is separating strong players from streaky players right now.

**Q: Which players are safest first unlocks after the NBA The Run update?**

A: Start with a balanced creator and a defensive counterpick. Players with reliable passing, stamina, and team-oriented badges usually provide better value early than niche specialists.

**Q: Are Street Legends worth using right now?**

A: They can be strong in specific setups, but many have sharp weaknesses (like defense or speed). Use them when your teammates can cover those gaps, not as blind default picks.
