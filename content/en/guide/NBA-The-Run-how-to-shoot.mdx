<Section icon={Swords} title="Executing Different Shot Types">
In NBA The Run, understanding how to perform various shot types is fundamental to becoming a versatile scorer. From long-range bombs to intricate finishes at the rim, each shot requires specific execution and situational awareness.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Jump Shots and Three-Pointers">
    For jump shots, timing is paramount. Wait for the player's shooting animation to reach its peak before releasing the shot button. Good spacing from defenders is crucial for higher accuracy. Practice in Shootaround mode to get a feel for each player's unique shot timing. Stepback threes can create space, but require precise execution.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Layups and Floaters">
    Driving to the basket for a layup or floater often involves navigating traffic. Press the shot button while driving towards the hoop. You can sometimes change a dunk into a layup mid-air to avoid a block, a key skill for adapting to defensive pressure. Utilize spin moves to create angles for easier finishes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Dunks and Alley-Oops">
    Dunks are high-percentage shots when you have a clear path to the basket. Hold down the shot button while driving. Alley-oops require teamwork and precise passing. Pass the ball to a teammate near the basket while they are cutting or jumping, then have them press the shoot button for an aerial finish. These are often momentum-building plays.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Utilizing In the Zone Abilities">
    When your player enters "In the Zone," their signature abilities activate, significantly boosting their performance. For shooters, this might mean increased shot accuracy, faster release, or an extended shooting range. Use these moments strategically, especially in clutch situations, to guarantee buckets.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Shot Selection Matters">
Attempting highly contested shots, especially from long range, will significantly reduce your success rate. Prioritize open looks and drives to the basket for higher percentage scoring opportunities.
</Callout>
